# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Architects and interior designers sourcing outdoor art for client projects: terraces, rooftop gardens, pool decks. They specify materials by weight, finish, and longevity. They know the difference between a product and an object. Secondary audience: affluent homeowners renovating an outdoor space with an architect's sensibility, not a decorator's budget.

## Product Purpose

KROMA sells circular aluminum wall art in three diameters (60 / 80 / 100 cm) as a limited edition collection. Each size is capped at 50 pieces. The page must make a professional specifier confident enough to contact KROMA for a project quote — and make the collector secure enough to commit to an object that will outlast the terrace it hangs on.

## Brand Personality

Precise. Undemonstrative. Enduring.

The voice is that of an object that needs no explanation, only context. No excitement. No urgency. Statements, not pitches. French-language copy carries the register naturally; translate the same restraint to every visual decision.

## Anti-references

- **Generic art e-commerce** (Saatchi Art, Society6): grid-of-products layout, price-forward, no editorial voice, photographs against white backgrounds. KROMA is not a marketplace.
- **Belgian craft clichés**: rough linen textures, handmade-rustic aesthetics, earthy warm tones, artisanal cottage feel. KROMA is industrial precision made beautiful, not a pottery studio.

## Design Principles

1. **Material confidence.** Let the aluminum speak. Photography over illustration. Silence over decoration. White space is not emptiness — it is the material's context.
2. **Edition logic.** Limited runs of 50 per size are a structural fact, not a marketing hook. Every design decision should reflect scarcity without performing it.
3. **Specifier trust.** An architect needs to know: What is it made of? How does it weather? Who made it? Answer those questions directly, without preamble.
4. **Scale as content.** The three diameters are not SKUs — they are a proportional system. The design must make that system visible and meaningful.
5. **Restraint over richness.** One element of motion, one typographic surprise, one moment of color. Everything else earns its place by doing nothing that distracts from the object.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Reduced motion support required (hero fade and scroll animations must degrade gracefully). French-language primary; no ARIA labels or alt text in placeholder state — these need real values before launch.
